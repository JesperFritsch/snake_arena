# Acceptable Use Policy

**Effective date:** <EFFECTIVE_DATE>

This Acceptable Use Policy ("AUP") sets out what you may not do when using gridsnake (the "Service"). It is incorporated into the Terms of Service by reference. Violating this AUP is a breach of the Terms and may result in immediate termination of your account, removal of your Submissions, and — where appropriate — reporting to law-enforcement or other authorities.

The Service exists to run snake-playing agents in a sandbox. Anything you submit must be in furtherance of that purpose. If you are unsure whether something is allowed, ask us at **jesperf96@gmail.com** before submitting.

## 1. Prohibited content in Submissions

You may not submit code, files, or other material that:

- contains malware, worms, ransomware, rootkits, or any other malicious software;
- is intended to exploit, probe for, or take advantage of vulnerabilities in the Service, in the sandbox (gVisor, Docker, the host kernel), in third-party services we rely on, or in any system outside the Service;
- attempts to read, modify, or interfere with the state of another user's account, project, Submissions, or matches;
- attempts to leak secrets, credentials, environment variables, host paths, or other operator data out of the sandbox;
- infringes third-party intellectual-property rights — including code you do not have the right to submit;
- contains personal data of other people without a lawful basis to process it;
- is illegal under Swedish or EU law, or under the law of your country of residence.

## 2. Prohibited use of the Service

You may not use the Service to:

- attempt to escape the sandbox or otherwise gain unauthorised access to the host, the network, or other tenants;
- use the platform as command-and-control infrastructure, a proxy, a scraper, a crypto miner, a DDoS source, a spam relay, or any other purpose unrelated to playing snake;
- exhaust shared resources beyond your fair share — including by spawning excessive build or match jobs, by submitting deliberately wasteful code, or by circumventing the per-step CPU and wall-clock budgets;
- evade rate limits, quotas, or technical controls, including by creating multiple accounts;
- reverse-engineer, scrape, or systematically download the Service beyond the public match data offered for that purpose (if any);
- harass, defame, or threaten other users — including through usernames, project names, code comments, or messages;
- impersonate any person or misrepresent your affiliation with any person or organisation;
- interfere with the operation of the Service in any other way.

## 3. Specifically permitted things

To avoid confusion, the following are explicitly **permitted**:

- Writing strong, even adversarial, agents — including ones that exploit specific weaknesses of opposing agents. The match rules are the contract; anything that conforms to them is fair play.
- Using third-party open-source libraries in your Submissions, provided their licenses permit it and you comply with their terms.
- Discussing strategies and sharing code publicly outside the Service.

## 4. Reporting abuse

If you believe another user is violating this AUP, or if you become aware of a security issue in the Service or its sandbox, email **jesperf96@gmail.com** with as much detail as you can share. We appreciate good-faith vulnerability reports and will not pursue legal action against researchers who:

- act in good faith, only against their own accounts and Submissions;
- avoid disrupting other users or destroying data they do not own;
- give us a reasonable opportunity to fix issues before public disclosure.

## 5. Enforcement

We may, at our sole discretion and without prior notice:

- inspect Submissions and match traffic for compliance with this AUP;
- refuse to build or run any Submission;
- delete Submissions, replays, or other data;
- terminate accounts;
- block IP addresses or other identifiers;
- preserve and disclose data to the extent required by law or reasonably necessary to investigate violations, protect the Service, or protect other users.

We will exercise these powers proportionately. Where a violation appears unintentional and low-risk, we will normally contact you before taking destructive action; where it does not, we will not.

## 6. Changes

We may update this AUP from time to time. Material changes will be announced on the Service at least 14 days before they take effect, unless an immediate change is required for legal or security reasons.
